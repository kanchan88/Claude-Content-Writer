P. gingivalis — the primary gum disease pathogen — has been found in brain tissue of Alzheimer's patients, releasing gingipains that disrupt the blood-brain barrier and promote tau hyperphosphorylation.
**Hook Direction:** "Gum disease bacteria have been found in the brains of Alzheimer's patients. Not in the gut. Not in the bloodstream. In the brain tissue itself."
**Why it works:** Executives fear cognitive decline above all. This connects a mundane health domain (dental) to their #1 fear with mechanism specificity that can't be dismissed.
**Source:** Nature npj Dementia 2025, Frontiers in Immunology 2025
