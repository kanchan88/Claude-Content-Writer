### Comment 2 — Value-Added Angle

Here's a simple check you can do right now:

Do your gums bleed when you brush or floss?

Most people think that's normal. It's not.

Bleeding gums = active infection. That bacteria doesn't stay in your mouth. It enters your bloodstream every time you chew, brush, or even sleep.

Three things worth asking your dentist at your next visit:

→ Do I have any signs of gum disease?
→ Has my bone level changed on X-rays?
→ Should I be screened for airway issues?

These three questions cost nothing. But they might catch what your annual physical never will.

### Comment 3 — Science Angle

For those who want to go deeper on this:

The bacterium is called Porphyromonas gingivalis. Researchers found it not just near the brain — but inside brain tissue of Alzheimer's patients.

It produces enzymes called gingipains that destroy nerve cells.

The study was published in Nature's npj Dementia journal in 2025. And it's not alone — over 20 studies now link chronic oral infection to cognitive decline, cardiovascular disease, and even pregnancy complications.

The mouth-body connection isn't alternative medicine.

It's becoming one of the most researched areas in modern science. Most people just haven't heard about it yet.
