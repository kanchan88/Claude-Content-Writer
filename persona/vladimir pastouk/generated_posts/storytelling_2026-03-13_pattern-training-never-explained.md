# Topic: After 15 Years, I Saw a Pattern My Training Never Explained

---

After 15 years, I saw a pattern my training never explained.

It started with a baby who couldn't breastfeed.

Then an executive on CPAP — still waking up exhausted every morning.

Then a woman with infections that came back no matter how many rounds of antibiotics she took.

Different people. Different symptoms. Different specialists.

Same dead end.

And every time, the trail led back to one place nobody had checked.

The tongue. The airway. The tissue underneath.

Nothing in dental school prepared me for this.

No textbook. No professor. No colleague could explain what I was seeing.

Just a pattern — repeating, quietly, year after year.

You start doubting yourself.

Maybe you're wrong. Maybe you're seeing connections that aren't there.

Then a child finally latches after you release a tongue-tie.

An executive's brain fog lifts after airway treatment.

Chronic headaches disappear when you address a restriction everyone else missed.

You stop doubting.

And you start building.

That's how Aligness was born.

Not from a boardroom. Not from a business plan.

From 15 years of evidence that my profession hadn't caught up to yet.

Aligness is an executive health advisory I co-founded with Dr. Heather Skeens — a corneal surgeon who saw the same gaps from the other side of medicine.

She went upstream. I went outward. Together, we built what should have existed all along — a private, physician-led advisory that connects the dots between your mouth, your airway, your sleep, and your performance.

Because no one should have to see 12 specialists before someone finally checks the one system that ties it all together.

Sometimes the patients write the textbook before the schools do.
