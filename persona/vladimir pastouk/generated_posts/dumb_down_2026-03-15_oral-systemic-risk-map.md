# Topic: 4 pathways from your mouth to your brain, heart, gut, and lungs

---

4 specialists. 4 organs.

Zero of them checking the one thing that connects all four.

Here's the simple version:

Your mouth doesn't just hold your teeth. It's connected to your brain, your heart, your gut, and your lungs.

Not poetically. Physically. Through your blood and your breath.

Here are the 4 pathways:

**Mouth → Brain**

Bacteria from infected gums enters your bloodstream.
It crosses into your brain.
Once there, it triggers inflammation.
That inflammation is linked to Alzheimer's and long-term cognitive decline.

Your neurologist will never check your gums.

**Mouth → Heart**

The same bacteria attaches to your arterial walls.
It triggers chronic vascular inflammation.
People with gum disease carry 2-3x the risk of heart attack and stroke.

Your cardiologist will never check your gums.

**Mouth → Gut**

You swallow oral bacteria every single day.
When that bacteria is harmful, it disrupts your gut microbiome.
That disruption is linked to IBS, systemic inflammation, and weakened immunity.

Your gastroenterologist will never check your gums.

**Mouth → Lungs**

Every breath you take can carry oral bacteria into your lungs.
It colonizes your lower respiratory tract.
It raises your risk of pneumonia and worsens COPD and asthma outcomes.

Your pulmonologist will never check your gums.

4 pathways. 4 organs. One entry point nobody is watching.

The mouth is not a separate organ.

It's the front door to the entire body.

And right now, for most people, that door is wide open.

## Self Comments

### 1. Personal Angle
After 30 years in practice, the thing that still surprises me is the look on a patient's face when they hear this for the first time.

Not shock. Relief.

Because they've been bouncing between specialists for years and nobody connected the dots. They weren't crazy. They just had a blind spot nobody told them about.

### 2. Trend / Thought
Medicine is moving toward systems thinking. Slowly.

But right now, your cardiologist doesn't talk to your dentist. Your neurologist doesn't ask about your gums. Your gastroenterologist doesn't screen your oral microbiome.

We built healthcare around organs. The mouth doesn't fit neatly into any specialty. That's exactly why it gets missed.

### 3. Science / Data
The bacterium most linked to gum disease, Porphyromonas gingivalis, has been found in three places it should never be: arterial plaque, brain tissue of Alzheimer's patients, and the gut microbiome of people with inflammatory bowel conditions.

One bacterium. Three organ systems. Same entry point.

(Dominy et al., Science Advances, 2019 / Arimatsu et al., Journal of Oral Microbiology, 2014)

### 4. Post Value Amplification
The line that matters most in this post:

"Your neurologist will never check your gums."

Not because they don't care. Because the system was never built to connect these two things. The mouth sits between specialties. Nobody owns it. And that's exactly why it's the biggest blind spot in modern healthcare.
