# Topic: Biohacking community misses the most crucial physiology — the airway

---

The biohacking community obsesses over supplements and trackers.

But misses the most crucial physiology in the human body — the airway.

Cold plunges. Red light therapy. HRV monitors. $400/month supplement stacks.

All downstream interventions.

None of them address the upstream driver most biohackers have never evaluated:

The oral airway.

→ A restricted tongue reduces airway diameter → oxygen delivery drops → sleep architecture collapses → no supplement stack compensates for that.

→ Oral microbiome imbalance drives systemic inflammation through the same pathways biohackers spend thousands trying to reduce with anti-inflammatory protocols.

→ Nitric oxide — the molecule every performance optimizer chases — is produced primarily in the nasal sinuses and oral cavity. Mouth breathing bypasses it entirely.

You can track your HRV religiously.

You can cold plunge every morning at 5am.

You can optimize every biomarker on your blood panel.

But if your airway is compromised, you're optimizing around an obstruction.

After 30 years of clinical practice, the pattern is unmistakable:

The most foundational variable in human performance is the one no wearable measures and no biohacking protocol includes.

You can't out-biohack a blocked airway.

## Self Comments

### Comment 1 (Technical)

The nitric oxide piece is what most people miss entirely. Your paranasal sinuses produce ~25% of your body's NO. Nasal breathing delivers it directly to the lungs where it vasodilates pulmonary arteries and improves oxygen exchange. Mouth breathers bypass this completely — and then wonder why their SpO2 looks fine but they still feel terrible. The airway isn't just about obstruction. It's about chemistry.

I spent 15 years watching patients come in after seeing 8, 10, sometimes 12 specialists. Nobody had checked their airway. Nobody had evaluated tongue function. The moment we addressed the oral structure — sleep improved, brain fog lifted, inflammation markers dropped. That pattern repeated enough times that I stopped calling it coincidence and started building a system around it. That's what became Glossodontics.
