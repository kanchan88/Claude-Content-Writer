## The Question That Started Everything

For 25 years, Dr. Heather Skeens stood in the operating room performing corneal transplants — replacing tissue that had failed.

One question haunted her: **Why did the tissue fail in the first place?**

That question pulled her out of the OR and upstream into a discovery that changed everything she believed about medicine.

The problem wasn't the doctors. It wasn't the technology. It wasn't the research.

**The problem was the architecture.**

Medicine was built to treat organs in isolation. But your body doesn't work in isolation. Your gut talks to your brain. Your hormones talk to your immune system. Your oral health talks to your heart.

And nobody — not one person on your medical team — is listening to the whole conversation.

*Unshackled* is the book that exposes why.

## What This Book Will Show You

### 1. Why "Normal Labs" Don't Mean You're Fine

Your bloodwork came back normal. Your doctor says you're healthy.

But you feel worse than you did five years ago. Brain fog. Afternoon crashes. Sleep that doesn't restore.

Dr. Skeens explains the gap between **"normal" ranges** (designed to detect disease) and **functional ranges** (designed to detect decline before disease). Your labs may say you're not sick. They don't say you're well.

→ What you'll learn: The markers that reveal what standard panels miss — and what "optimal" actually looks like.

---

### 2. Why Your 5 Specialists Don't Talk to Each Other (And What It's Costing You)

You have a cardiologist. An endocrinologist. A gastroenterologist. A functional medicine doctor. Maybe a sleep specialist.

**None of them have spoken to each other in the last 12 months.**

Only 49% of primary care doctors communicate with the specialists they refer patients to. You are the only person with the full clinical picture — carrying context from one appointment to the next like a project manager who never applied for the job.

Dr. Skeens calls this **the coordination gap** — and it's the single most dangerous failure in executive healthcare.

→ What you'll learn: Why fragmented care isn't a quality problem — it's a structural one. And what coordinated health architecture actually looks like.

### 3. Why Executive Physicals Give You Data Without Direction

You paid $5,000–$10,000 for a comprehensive executive physical. You got 80 biomarkers, a full-body scan, and a bound PDF.

Then you went home.

No action plan. No coordination. No follow-up strategy. No one connecting your cardiac risk markers to your cortisol patterns to your inflammatory load.

**A PDF is not a health plan.**

Dr. Skeens breaks down the difference between **health evaluation** (what executive physicals do) and **health advisory** (what you actually need) — and why the gap between them is where most executives get lost.

→ What you'll learn: The 5 questions your executive physical should answer but doesn't — and what to demand next time.

### 7. What Physician Burnout Means for Your Care

More than half of US physicians report burnout. Not the "I had a long week" kind — the kind where the frontal cortex physically thins, empathy erodes, and clinical decision-making degrades.

Your doctor is operating inside a system that optimizes for throughput, not outcomes. 15-minute appointments. Insurance-dictated protocols. Charting that takes longer than the visit itself.

Dr. Skeens names the systemic drivers of physician burnout — and explains why it directly affects the quality of care you receive, even from the best doctors.

→ What you'll learn: Why the best individual doctors aren't enough when the system they operate in is architecturally broken — and what an alternative model looks like.
